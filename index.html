Abstract

        Smart materials are materials that react due to an environmental change such as temperature, light, electricity and pressure. When reacting to these  changes they may change shape or colour and in 
        some cases even produce an electrical current. Some examples of these materials are shape memory alloys (SMAs), piezoelectric materials and self-healing polymers. Smart materials are used in various 
        industries due to their versatility and wide range of materials. They are incredibly useful to designers and engineers who need a material that can improve a product and increase the product’s life span. 
        They’re continuously evolving alongside new technologies and are being used more in industry. The hope is that smart materials will be used more to push forwards a more sustainable and non-polluting environment. 
        As a result of this, pollution would reduce and companies and consumers carbon footprint would reduce, which will help reduce the concerning effects of global warming.
