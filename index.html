Abstract

Smart materials are materials that react due to an environmental change such as temperature, light, electricity and pressure. When reacting to these  
changes, they may change shape or colour and in some cases even produce an electrical current. Some examples of these materials are; shape memory alloys 
(SMAs), piezoelectric materials and self-healing polymers. Smart materials are used in various industries due to their versatility and wide range of 
materials. They are incredibly useful to designers and engineers who need a material that can improve a product and increase the product’s life span. 
They’re continuously evolving alongside new technologies and are being used more in industry. The hope is that smart materials will be used more to push 
forwards a more sustainable and non-polluting environment. As a result of this, pollution would reduce and companies and consumers carbon footprint would 
reduce, which will help reduce the concerning effects of global warming.

Introduction

Smart materials are materials that have the ability to respond to changes in their environment. Examples of smart materials are shape memory
alloys (SMAs), piezoelectric materials and thermochromic coatings. They are used in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, 
construction and healthcare. Certain smart materials only react to certain changes in their environment and they react when exposed to these 
environmental changes. Reactions that occur are, change in shape, change in colour, and producing an electrical current. The use of smart materials 
could aid companies and consumers to reduce waste, save costs and improve performance. They also may help to save energy and materials, for example in 
the construction industry temperature-sensitive glass may be used to save energy. As technologies evolve these materials will advance and become more 
efficient and be used more in industry which will hopefully promote a more sustainable and eco-friendly environment in the future. This will help 
companies and consumers to save money and reduce waste that could harm the environment.

Analysis and discussion

What are smart materials?

Smart materials are materials that detect changes in their environment and react to these changes in a constructive manner. The reactions that may occur 
involve; changing form (shape), colour, or producing an electrical current.There are many different types of smart materials and different ones react 
differently to a change in their environment. A shape memory alloy is a material which can return to its original shape when a change to its environment 
occurs an example is nitinol, which is an alloy of nickel and titanium, and if it is deformed, heat is applied to it to change it back to its original shape.
Piezoelectric materials generate electricity when a force is applied to them, this force includes either stretching or compressive forces. When electricity is applied to 
them they also change their shape. Thermochromic materials change colour based on the temperature. Finally,self-healing polymers which are polymers that 
can repair themselves, to a certain degree, if they are deformed or broken. This will help to increase a product's life span and save costs as the product 
will not need to be replaced as often.


Applications of smart materials in industry:

Medical: Nitinol is used in dentistry for wires for braces where the body temperature in the patient's mouth changes the nitinol back to the perfect arch 
that the material was pre-set to. As the nitinol reverts back to its original shape, it applies a light pressure to the patients teeth which, in time, will 
straighten the patients teeth to their desired preference. 
Automotive: Piezoelectric materials are used in the automotive industry for things like sensors.“For instance, piezoelectric material sensors are used to 
measure different qualities such as pressure, acceleration and vibration” '(Reddy et al., 2024).' What makes them good for sensors is that they are 
very sensitive which allows for little changes such as a pressure change to be noticed fast by the operator. They also do not need a power source, which
saves electricity and allows the batteries to last longer in automotive vehicles
Aerospace: Thermochromic materials may possibly be used in spacecraft by NASA, which is a well renowned space agency that has been around for nearly 60 
years. NASA has created some design targets that their thermochromic variable emittance coatings (VECs) will achieve such as emitting thermal (heat) 
energy to reduce energy consumption for space vehicles and also reducing mass of space vehicles as more simplistic cooling system would be used instead. 
'(Peoples et al., 2021).'
Construction: Self-healing polymers are used in construction by putting them into concrete. By doing this the polymers could help to repair small cracks 
in the concrete which would help to further reinforce it and increase the concrete’s life span.'(Choi et al., 2023).'. This sort of concrete is an adaptation 
of pre-existing self-healing concrete used by the Romans over 2000 years ago. Notably, one of the most famous uses of their self-healing concrete is the 
Pantheon, which is the largest unreinforced concrete dome currently, even though it is over 2000 years old. The reason we do not currently use this sort 
of concrete is that it is not strong and durable enough for modern day construction.
